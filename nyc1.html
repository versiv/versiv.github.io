October 11th I’ve not flown in years, and have avoided major interstate travel since the pandemic… Everything seems so frenetic, so empty-of-substance and over-torqued with protocol. Living in a constant state of mindless panic, self-constructed and self-deluded. We scurry around from sign post to sign post, in need of direction but listening for none. What are we without a lead, a leash, some bullring through our nose for the pace-maker to tug as we abandon our gut-compass and sacrifice our morality for the next vogue status quo… It’s unsurprising how much a ginger-ale and pack of crackers can level-out the mood of a plane crammed with passengers. There’s no real reason for me to write, as in the face of the moment I am at a loss for real words… What is “real”? What is anything aside from an arbitrary sense of systematic deterioration, a way to conduct the traffic of linguistic rush-hour, to make some sense to the senseless, or to sense less? Writing is supposed to be cathartic, empowering, a window to the inner-mono-poly-dialogue that creeps up in one’s belly, deep down viscera. It’s difficult to find inspiration on a plane, where the psycho-sonicsphere is riddled with inconsistent 909 crashes, wailings of unhappy babes, and so forth. The lack of consistent connection to the outside world is claustrophobic, wherein the wi-fi portal may the only portal to the grounded reality.
